Sketch Guy Interactive

As the web editor of The New York Times' personal finance section, I built a gallery to serve as an index of the work of a columnist who is also a sketch artist. A developer built a template to my specifications using CSS; I populated it and updated it with new entries.

Egyptian Revolution Skype Interviews

When the Egyptian revolution began, I used Twitter to identify activists and independent journalists working on the ground. I recorded video interviews and edited them as part of our breaking news coverage.

Your Money Athlete Slide Show

To accompany a column about sports stars who experience financial hardships, I reported, wrote, and photo-edited a gallery. I selected a template that was familiar to readers for the simplest possible user experience.

Voices of Student Debt

As part of a series on the student loan crisis, I wanted to feature voices of students who struggled with education debt while demonstrating the scale and characterizing the sources of their debt. Working with a reporter who wrote an accompanying article, I identified students in a single community, conducted interviews, sent a photographer to take portraits, and built an interactive gallery that demonstrated how the students felt about their circumstances and how the problem had become so entrenched.

Your Money Special Section Audio Slide Show

The New York Times' personal finance desk collaborated with the American Public Media show Marketplace Money to produce semiannual special sections in print, corresponding with show specials. We hosted audio from the show on our website as stand-alone pieces, paired with articles, and I produced audio slide shows. I assigned photographers for select stories and combined them with the audio to provide a richer audience experience.

Small Business: Introducing Make Your Pitch

To respond to an engaged readership's interest in dialogue with reporters and editors, I created a contest for small business owners to receive expert input on their startups. I conceived, assigned, published and promoted a video made by a dedicated video producer announcing the contest and recruiting entries.

Make Your Pitch Gallery

Winning entries of the Make Your Pitch contest were published on the Small Business blog, along with a response from an entrepreneurship expert. I compiled those entries in an interactive gallery as a compendium for readers to browse and access all the videos and the feedback they received.

Detroit Auto Show Interactive Gallery

At the 2013 Detroit Auto Show, I compiled the Autos desk's coverage of the show's highlights into an interactive gallery, allowing users to sort cars by grouping and brand. I assigned photographers at the show to take photos of rotating cars, which I made into GIFs. I worked with developers to design this template and populated it from the floor of the auto show as stories and photos were filed.

The Upshot: Baseball Nation

A team of reporters, graphics designers and multimedia programmers worked together to build an interactive map showcasing data we recieved from Facebook. I acted as a showrunner for the entire project, shepherding the concept through production, coordinating with other desks, planning promotion through social media and building this special page in such a way that it could be referenced across the site.

Reporter Q&A

In order to boost audience engagement and provide transparency for data-heavy stories, I utilized social media to host interactions between readers and reporters. On Twitter and Facebook, I announced that reporters would address a given story in more detail, solicited reader questions and published reporters' responses.

Student Loan Repayment Calculator

The business desk's coverage of the student loan crisis demonstrated the need for specific and personalizable service journalism. I conceived and developed this calculator, licensed third-party data, identified the necessary inputs and outputs, worked with programmers to build an intuitive user interface and built a page that could be referenced across the site.

Rent or Buy Calculator

This calculator has long stood as one of the most popular features on the New York Times' site due to its ease-of-use and utility. In response to a changing economy and the development of more functionalities, I oversaw a total redesign of the calculator that included more possible inputs in a more user-friendly interface, coordinating with graphic designers, other desks, and social media editors to maximize its reach.

Upshot Newsletter

With other editors, I designed a format for a weekly newsletter featuring the best of The Upshot as well as exclusive content. I created modules soliciting readers' email addresses, developed a layout, contributed writing to round-up pieces, worked with the advertising department, built a weekly issue and sent it to subscribers. It quickly became one of The New York Times' most popular newsletters.

Upshot Senate Model

A team of reporters, graphics designers and multimedia programmers worked together to build a model of national election results based on polling data from multiple firms. Producing it required me to work with programmers to create a custom interactive format, coordinate with other desks, plan promotion through social media and build promos for use on the Upshot section front and elsewhere on the New York Times site.

Social Video

As an editor for the University of Southern California's Annenberg Media Center, I have taught students how to make social videos using the organization's template and branding, with a focus on producing high-quality content with strong visuals and narratives. For this piece, students solicited stories on Twitter, conducted reporting and produced this video.

Native Social Storytelling

As the staff editor for Annenberg Media Center's special projects desk, I have encouraged students to innovate beyond using social media to tell breaking news stories to develop features for Twitter, Facebook, Snapchat and other social formats. In this instance, students wanted to celebrate Black History month by acknowledging the accomplishments of their predecessors at USC. They combined traditional reporting skills, including finding archival photographs, with a newer medium.

Student Government Elections Issues Interactive

As a piece of service journalism, special projects sought to simplify the platforms of the various candidates for student government offices. We compared similar interactives from professional news organizations and built this page as simply as possible, allowing students to code the page themselves.
